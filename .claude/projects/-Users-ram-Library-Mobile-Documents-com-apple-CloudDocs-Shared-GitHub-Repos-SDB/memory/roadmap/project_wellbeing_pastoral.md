**Priority:** Should-have. Growing importance post-COVID. Compass leads here.

**What it is:**
A module for tracking student wellbeing, safeguarding concerns, and pastoral interventions — distinct from behaviour management (which tracks incidents). This tracks the whole-child view: emotional state, risk factors, support plans, and outcomes.

**What EduPod should build:**
- Wellbeing notes: confidential per-student records with tiered access (not visible to all staff)
- Safeguarding alerts: flag concerns with permissioned visibility (designated liaison person, principal only)
- Intervention plans: define support actions, assign responsible staff, track progress, record outcomes
- Intervention cost tracking (for resource allocation)
- Wellbeing check-ins: periodic surveys or teacher observations
- Cross-data correlation: link wellbeing to attendance patterns, behaviour incidents, grade changes
- Referral tracking: external agency referrals (CAMHS, Tusla, counselling services)
- Wellbeing dashboard: school-wide indicators, at-risk students, trend analysis
- Children First Act 2015 compliance: child protection procedures, designated liaison person records

**Competitor status:**
- Compass: Chronicle (behaviour + safeguarding), Digest (wellbeing alerts), Interventions (support plans with cost tracking). This is one of their strongest selling points.
- VSware: no dedicated wellbeing module
- Tyro: unknown

**Why it matters:**
- Post-COVID wellbeing is a national priority in Irish education
- The Department of Education's Wellbeing Policy Statement requires schools to monitor and support student wellbeing
- Schools currently track this in spreadsheets, paper files, or not at all
- Permissioned access is critical — safeguarding data must be restricted to designated persons
- This data feeds into ETB analytics (cross-school wellbeing trends)

**How to apply:** Build after behaviour management (they share data models and UI patterns). The behaviour module handles what happened; the wellbeing module handles why and what support is needed. Together they provide the complete pastoral picture.
