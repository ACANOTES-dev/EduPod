**Priority:** Non-negotiable for formal market entry. This is a process/business task, not a code task.

**What it is:**
- The SMIS Framework is managed by HEAnet (Ireland's national education and research network) on behalf of the Department of Education
- Multi-supplier, multi-lot framework covering primary, community national, and post-primary schools
- Up to 5 approved suppliers per lot
- Schools procure through mini-competitions among approved suppliers
- Compliant with OGP (Office of Government Procurement) rules

**Why it matters:**
- Without SMIS approval, every school purchase is a riskier procurement decision for the school
- The framework legitimises you alongside Compass, VSware, and Tyro
- ETBs in particular are likely to require SMIS-approved suppliers
- Compass was approved May 2025 — this was a significant milestone for their Irish entry

**What's required:**
- Application when the next framework round opens
- Likely need: proven product, Irish data residency, GDPR compliance, statutory reporting capabilities, security certifications
- May need reference customers (pilot schools)

**How to apply:** Monitor HEAnet for framework openings. Build statutory integrations (P-POD, Tusla, DES Returns) first — these are likely prerequisites. Having 3-5 pilot schools running before applying strengthens the application significantly.
