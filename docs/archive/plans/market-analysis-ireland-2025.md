# Irish School MIS Market — Competitive Analysis

**Date:** March 2025
**Scope:** Compass Education, VSware, and EduPod competitive positioning in the Irish school MIS market

---

## 1. Market Landscape

| Metric                      | Value                        |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Primary schools             | ~3,200                       |
| Post-primary schools        | ~722                         |
| Total students              | ~967,000                     |
| Annual ICT grant per school | €2,000 base + ~€25/pupil     |
| Education budget (2025)     | €11.8 billion                |
| Digital Strategy funding    | €200M committed through 2027 |

**Market structure:** A duopoly-plus. Aladdin owns ~90% of primary. VSware owns ~55-60% of post-primary (declining). Compass and Tyro are the funded challengers. The government's SMIS Framework (managed by HEAnet) explicitly exists to break incumbent lock-in by enabling mini-competitions among approved suppliers.

**Key dynamic:** This market is actively fragmenting. VSware's co-founder left and built Tyro, which has already captured ~250 post-primary schools. Compass entered with EQT private equity backing. Schools are demonstrably willing to switch — the question is who they switch to.

## 2. Compass Education — Deep Profile

### What They Are

An Australian-founded (2009, Melbourne) school platform with 3,000+ schools globally, ~100+ in Ireland. Acquired by EQT (major PE firm) in January 2025. Revenue growing ~25% YoY, EBITDA growing 40%+ YoY. 200+ employees. Dublin office at 26 Fitzwilliam Street Upper, Dublin. SMIS Framework approved May 2025.

### What They Offer (40+ modules)

| **Student & Wellbeing** | Attendance, Chronicle (behaviour/safeguarding), Digest (wellbeing alerts), Interventions, Admissions                                                                                  |

| **Assessment**          | Pulse (KPIs), Summative/Formative tracking, Analytics, School Reports, Exams (scheduling, seating, clash resolution)                                                                  |
| **Communication**       | News Feed, SMS/Email/Push, Parent Portal, Parent Conferences, Events                                                                                                                  |
| **Finance**             | Payments (individualised billing), Online Canteen (cashless POS), SMS Pack                                                                                                            |
| **Admin**               | Statutory Returns, Calendar, Resource Bookings, Minutes, Issue Tracker, Professional Development, Single Central Record, TimeCard (staff clock-in), Kiosk (visitor sign-in with RFID) |

### What They Don't Offer

- No dedicated gradebook/standards-based grading system
- No rubric-based assessment
- No GPA calculation
- No payroll/HR
- No report card generation with approval workflows
- No finance module beyond basic payments (no invoicing, credit notes, late fees, scholarships)
- No GDPR compliance request management
- No CMS for school websites
- No admissions form builder
- No academic transcripts
- No multi-language UI (uses Google Translate as a bolt-on)

### Strengths

1. **Breadth of modules** — 40+ modules means a school rarely needs to go elsewhere
2. **Timetabling (Griddle)** — consistently praised; handles complex Irish cycle spreads
3. **Wellbeing/Safeguarding (Chronicle)** — strongest in the market for pastoral care tracking
4. **Physical touchpoints** — Kiosk (RFID reception), Canteen (cashless POS) — tangible, visible value
5. **PE-backed growth machine** — EQT money means aggressive sales, migration support, "pay nothing until your contract ends" switching offer
6. **PPOD + Tusla integration** — automated daily sync, CBA upload
7. **Irish data residency** — data hosted onshore in Ireland
8. **Support** — AI agent + LiveChat + phone, structured 6-stage onboarding

### Weaknesses

1. **Mobile app quality** — 3.7/5 Google Play (27K reviews), iOS complaints about broken keyboards, random logouts, payment flows requiring re-entry
2. **UI inconsistency** — some modules modern, others visibly legacy. Teachers describe it as "terrible UI" and "clunky"
3. **New to Ireland** — only ~100 schools. Limited local reference base. Integration partners are UK-MAT-focused
4. **Opaque pricing** — no published rates; modular upsell means costs escalate
5. **Assessment is shallow** — no real gradebook. Formative/summative tracking but no rubrics, standards, GPA, grade curves, AI grading
6. **No payroll** — schools still need a separate payroll solution
7. **No genuine bilingual support** — bolted-on Google Translate, not a first-class i18n system
8. **Glassdoor: 2.5/5** — 32% recommend, 24% positive outlook. Internal problems potentially affecting product velocity
9. **Zero independent reviews** — no Trustpilot, no EdTech Impact, no Capterra reviews. All testimonials are self-curated

## 6. Irish Statutory Reporting Requirements

Schools must report to multiple government bodies. Any MIS entering the Irish market must support all of these:

### POD (Primary Online Database)

- Nationwide individualised database of all primary school pupils
- Maintained by the Department of Education
- Used for: National School Annual Census, teacher allocation, capitation grants
- Schools submit returns annually in October

### P-POD (Post-Primary Online Database)

- Central database for post-primary student and school data
- Accessed via the Department's Esinet secure portal
- Schools must generate October Returns (no later than 7 October)
- Junior Cycle CBA results and senior cycle data uploaded via P-POD

### Tusla (Education Welfare Services)

- Under the Education (Welfare) Act 2000:
  - **Student Absence Reports (SAR)**: Report all students absent 20+ days, submitted twice per year via Tusla Portal
  - **Annual Attendance Report (AAR)**: Total days lost through absence, submitted within 6 weeks of school year end
- Covers children aged 6-16

### Other Statutory Returns

- **DES Annual Returns**: Staff data, class data, subject data
- **September Returns**: Encrypted files (A, B, C, D, E) + Form TL for teacher hours
- **DTR Returns**: Subject code management
- **Child Protection**: Procedures under Children First Act 2015
- **GDPR**: Board of Management is the data controller

## 7. Market Dynamics & Decision Making

### Who Decides

- **Primary schools**: Principal recommends, Board of Management approves
- **Post-primary schools**: Principal + Deputy Principal, Board approves
- **ETB schools**: ETB can procure centrally for all their schools (10-20 at once)
- **Voluntary secondary**: JMB influences policy, individual Boards decide

### Switching Costs

- **Data migration**: Historical records must be exported and re-imported
- **Timing**: Switches typically happen over summer
- **POD/P-POD re-linking**: Must re-establish statutory connections
- **Staff retraining**: Teachers must learn a new system
- **Parent app migration**: Re-onboarding all parents
- **Report card templates**: Must rebuild customised templates
- **Contract lock-in**: Annual subscription cycles

### What Lowers Switching Costs

- SMIS Framework legitimises the switch process
- New entrants actively offer migration support
- Compass offers "pay nothing until your current contract ends"

### Key Pain Points for Irish Schools

- 66% of principals have considered stepping down (NPF 2025 survey) due to administrative overload
- Multiple disconnected systems for different functions
- Legacy UIs not mobile-friendly
- Limited parent engagement features
- Slow innovation from incumbents
- Schools in "dire financial situation" — every software cost is scrutinised

## 8. Strategic Recommendations

### Phase 1: Table Stakes (Must-Have Before Approaching Any Irish School)

1. **P-POD integration** — automated sync of student data + CBA results
2. **Tusla attendance reporting** — export format for 20-day absence reports + Annual Attendance Report
3. **September/DES Returns** — generate encrypted return files
4. **Behaviour management module** — incident logging, teacher-initiated, parent-visible, reports
5. **Irish locale (ga-IE)** — part of Phase 3 multilingual refactor

### Phase 2: Competitive Advantages to Lead With

1. **"The only MIS with a real gradebook"** — rubric-based grading, standards tracking, AI grading, grade curves. Senior Cycle reform demands continuous assessment. EduPod is the only platform ready.
2. **"Report cards that write themselves"** — template system + approval + AI comments + bilingual PDFs + multi-channel delivery. Unique in market.
3. **"Finance that actually works"** — full invoicing, credit notes, late fees, scholarships, payment plans. Replaces separate finance systems.
4. **"Every parent, every language"** — 10 languages natively. Polish mother reads in Polish, Syrian father reads in Arabic RTL, French-speaking family reads in French. No competitor can match this.
5. **"Payroll built in"** — neither competitor offers this. Consider integration with established Irish payroll provider to avoid compliance burden.

### Phase 3: Go-to-Market

**Target post-primary first:**

- Post-primary is the contested, fragmenting market
- More complex needs where EduPod's depth shines
- Larger schools = higher ARPU
- Primary is locked by Aladdin at 90%

**Entry strategy:**

1. **3-5 pilot schools** — free/discounted for a full academic year. Target schools unhappy with VSware.
2. **Irish language niche** — 29 Gaelcholaisti with no MIS supporting Irish. Small but defensible.
3. **SMIS Framework application** — legitimises you in government procurement.
4. **Case studies from pilots** — one enthusiastic principal influences a cluster via IPPN networks.
5. **Target ETB clusters** — win one ETB, win 10-20 schools at once.
6. **All-inclusive pricing** — price at parity (~€7,500-10,000/school/year), all modules included. No nickel-and-diming.

## 10. The Bottom Line

**EduPod can compete. The product is already deeper than both incumbents in the areas that matter most to daily school operations.**

The window is open: VSware is declining under absentee ownership. Compass is well-funded but has mediocre execution quality and no depth in assessment/gradebook/finance. Tyro is growing but narrow in scope. The SMIS Framework is designed to invite new suppliers. Irish principals are overworked and seeking relief.

The technical foundation is strong. The gap is Irish statutory compliance, market presence, and trust. Close those gaps and EduPod has a legitimate shot at the post-primary market — not as an alternative, but as the only architecturally multilingual, AI-native school platform with world-class gradebook, finance, and reporting built from scratch on modern infrastructure.
