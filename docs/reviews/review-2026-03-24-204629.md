## Executive Summary

Six parallel Opus 4.6 agents performed a hostile audit of ~180k lines across a multi-tenant school management SaaS (NestJS + Next.js + BullMQ) processing children's data in the EU. The codebase demonstrates strong fundamentals — RLS is correctly enforced with parameterised queries, JWT validation is thorough, RTL styling compliance is flawless (zero violations), translation parity is near-perfect (3,672/3,673 keys), and 1,193 tests exist with 2,188 assertions.

However, the audit surfaces **81 findings** including **8 P0 (launch-blocking)** issues concentrated in two areas: (1) GDPR compliance for children's data is structurally absent — no consent infrastructure, no retention policy, incomplete anonymisation, PII leaking to third parties, and (2) a live Redis password is committed to the git repository. The platform **cannot legally onboard tenants in Ireland without addressing the P0 GDPR findings.**

**Total: 8 P0 | 12 P1 | 24 P2 | 16 P3 | 21 P4**
