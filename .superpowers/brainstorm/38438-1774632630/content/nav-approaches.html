Three Futures for EduPod

Same product, three fundamentally different experiences. Click the one that makes your gut react. Don't overthink it.

A

The Spatial Workspace

No sidebar. No traditional nav. The dashboard IS the navigation.

Your homepage is a living workspace of cards — each one a domain (People, Academics, Finance...) showing live data. Click a zone to dive in. Navigate via the command palette (⌘K) or click back to the workspace. Like an iPad home screen that shows you what's happening inside each app before you open it.

No sidebar clutter Live data at a glance Feels genuinely new Learning curve Extra click to reach deep pages Arc + ClassDojo energy

B

The Refined Rail

Sidebar reimagined — icon rail + contextual panel. Familiar but elevated to premium.

A slim icon rail (always visible) with 8 domain icons. Hover or click to reveal a contextual sub-panel with the domain's pages. The sidebar never shows 30 items — it shows 8 icons, and expands only where you're focused. Think macOS Dock meets Stripe's sidebar. Familiar enough that nobody needs training, polished enough to feel new.

Zero learning curve Fast deep navigation Scales to any feature count Still a sidebar (visually) Less radical differentiation Apple + Stripe energy

C

The Hub Bar

Top navigation with domain hubs + contextual sub-tabs. No sidebar at all.

A horizontal hub bar at the top with 6-7 domain pills (Home, People, Learning, Operations, Finance, Reports, Settings). Below it, a contextual strip shows the pages within that hub. The homepage is a personalised command center. Feels like a modern web app (think Notion, Linear) but structured for schools. Bold enough to be different, familiar enough to be instant.

Full-width content area Familiar web-app pattern Scales gracefully Works perfectly on mobile Horizontal space limited ClassDojo + Linear energy
