What makes Option D unique

The Rail D

A permanent dark 64px icon rail on the left. Not a sidebar — it has no text, no expansion, no sub-menus. Just 7 icons + search + avatar. It's the thinnest possible global navigation that still gives you one-click access to every domain. The emerald accent bar on the active item tells you where you are at a glance.

Three-Column Home D

The extra width freed by having no top hub bar allows a true 3-column canvas: priorities (left), domain cards as a compact list (centre), and stats + quick actions (right). More information density without feeling cramped — because each column has a clear purpose.

Domain Cards as List D

Instead of a grid of cards (A) or a bento (B), domains are presented as a navigable list with icons, names, and live stats. Takes less space, shows more domains at once, and the chevron makes each row feel like a portal into that world. More like iOS Settings than a dashboard.

Best of All Worlds D

D takes the rail from B's dock concept and puts it on the side. Takes the tab-based module navigation from B and C. Takes the priority feed from C. And takes the domain cards from A. It's the most balanced — nothing extreme, nothing safe, just intentional.

D is the pragmatist's dream. The rail is always there but costs only 64px. Modules use tabs for full-width content. The home has density without clutter. It's the option that would feel most natural to an admin who lives in this app 8 hours a day — always oriented, never hunting, never overwhelmed. It's not the most dramatic option. It's the most livable one.
